Volleyball Positions

Setter
The setter is the mastermind of the team's offensive play. They create opportunities for their players to score points for the team. This position requires the player to play all around the court and is normally in the right front or right back position.
Outside Hitter
Also called wing spiker, left side hitter. This player is either positioned at the left front of middle back of the court. They are required to know how to receive, block, serve, and play offensively and defensively.
Middle Blocker
This player is situated in the middle front of the court. Their main purpose is to set up the block to stop the ball from getting over the net or allow the ball to be dug up from their defensive line. When a middle blocker is playing backrow, they are switched with the libero.
Opposite
Also called the wing spiker or right side hitter. This player plays on the right side of the court. Their duty is mostly to attack, block, and serve. Sometimes, they are switched off with the setter to run a schematic where the opposite and setter switches roles.
Libero
This position is responsible for playing in the backrow positions. Their main goal is to lead the defensive scheme. They are normally the most skilled in defense and passing.
